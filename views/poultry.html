POULTRY BREEDS (Commonly found in Nigeria)

ISA BROWN

The ISA Brown is a crossbreed of chicken. It is thought to have been the result of a complex series of crosses including but not limited to Rhode Island Reds and Rhode Island Whites. They are layers known for its high egg production of approximately 300 eggs per hen in the first year of laying. They are brown in colour and also lays brown coloured eggs. They are highly suitable with Nigerians weather conditions.

CORNISH CROSS BROILER

The Cornish Cross Broiler is the best meat chicken available. They gain weight rapidly (up to/above 2kg at 6 weeks) and have a good feed to weight ratio. They are white in colour.

RED BROILER

Red broilers, don't mature as fast as the Cornish crosses. It takes them twice as long, 12 weeks to reach market size. As their name implies, these birds have feathering of various shades of red, and some have dark tails.

RHODE ISLAND RED

The Rhode Island Red is an American breed of domestic chicken. It was a dual-purpose breed, raised both for meat and for eggs; modern strains have been bred for their egg laying abilities.
